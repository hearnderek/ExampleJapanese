“I’m still learning.” –Michelangelo

Software Development

The Pragmatic Programmer

Wisdom for any language. One readthrough isn't enough of this book.

The Mythical Man-Month

A classic. The examples used may seem outdated but it's painfully true. Anyone who manages a software project needs to understand the lessons in this book lest they waste everyone's time.

Japanese Learning

Japanese Childrens Dictionary

Once you can read at an intermediate level pick up one of these so your understanding of works will come directly from the language.

Childrens Kanji Dictionary

I want to know why a Kanji is written the way it is, but I also want it in plan English. I mean Japanese.

JLPT Test Prep

If you're studying for the JLPT these are great books to have on your shelf. Btw don't bother with anything below the N3.

Foundational Grammar

Japanese grammar is the hardest part of first learning the language. Tae Kim has a fantastic website, but I enjoyed having the book on my shelf.

General Language Learning

Fluent in 3 Months

No you won't be fluent in 3 months, but there's a lot of truth in this book. Go out and speak to people.

Improve Your Foreign Language Immediately

This book has a different approach then most. It honestly has sage advice for people at every level. When I moved it was one of the few books I took with me.

Investing

The Intelligent Investor

Understanding how to value a company before you decide to invest in it. Make sure you're investing and not just gambling.

Options Futures and Other Derivatives

If you're going to try your hand with Options here's a books that'll take you through the how and why. You'll gain more with a read through this than randomly browsing Investopedia.
